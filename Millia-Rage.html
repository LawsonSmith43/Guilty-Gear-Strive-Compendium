Millia Rage

Millia is elegant and calm, but does not hesitate to fight when necessary. Cold and somewhat silent, she was molded by years of assassin conditioning, with an upbringing apparently bereft of much affection. Of serene reactions that show little interest in anything, Millia doesn't feel at disadvantage for being a woman, and makes decisions solely on the basis of cost and benefit.[9] Yet under her cold exterior, she has not completely lost her humanity;[9] she is compassionate, especially to children—she cannot bring herself to harm them[, and believes the life of an assassin to be loathsome.

Perhaps because of her complex relationship with Zato—her savior, teacher, lover, and enemy, Millia seems to hold men in low regard, thinking them to be selfish, and is not afraid to voice her biting opinions of them. As for her decision to leave the Guild[ (which, no matter what, she sees as her home), Millia chose to do so after a greal deal of thinking, as she didn't want to "keep hating what she hated", and the only way out was betraying Zato. No matter her feelings for him, Millia prioritizes her own will above all, and wishes to find happiness. Settling her past, however, has led her to obsession at times.

After leaving the Guild[3] and living among ordinary people,[4] Millia remains a lonely individual, but she has learned to tap into a wider range of human emotion, making her more considerate of others, and more compassionate. She also makes sure the danger that follows her does not spread to others. When faced with a situations she doesn't know how to handle, she will act more like a child than her usual, detached self.
